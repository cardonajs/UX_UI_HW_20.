Project Overview

The Problem:Mental health continues to decline. With lack of free resources available, users do not have an outlet to safely vent, learn, and cope with daily stressors and hardships.

The Solution:Eunoia (Greek for “well mind” or ”beautiful thinking”) is a mobile app solution that allows users in close proximity to anonymously solicit and exchange visual expressions of encouragement (e.g. gifs and emojis of hugs, hearts, etc.) to uplift one another during difficult moments.

Research

In this research We planned and conducted six 1 to 1 interviews with potential users in the Washington, DC Metropolitan area and one survey.

User testing

Due to Covid restrictions all of our user testing was completed remote via zoom. Each group member conducted 2 user test in low/mid fidelity phase. A total of 12 user test were completed.

Key findings: Icons and word choice must be carefully thought of and certain words can confuse the user. Context is important, people who go into our application blind don’t necessarily envision it how we did. Take feedback with an open mind. For example the chat feature was something we didn’t think about during brainstorming.

Summary

In the future we would like to further develop the learn feature and add a mental health dictionary which would include a larger selection of conditions and illnesses to learn about. We also thing being this a mental health app we need to add resources for user such as hotlines and online resources To enhance the user experience we would like to customize a journal cover to the users likes and preferences - Color, stickers, images Lastly add a customizable feel good musical play list for users to listen to while journaling
